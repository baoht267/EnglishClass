Example (ASCII only):

Present Perfect: used for actions that happened in the past but are relevant now.
Example: I have lost my keys.

Past Simple: used for actions that finished at a specific time in the past.
Example: I lost my keys yesterday.

Common error: mixing "since" and "for".
Use "since" + a starting point (since 2020). Use "for" + a duration (for 3 years).
